Research continues into the psychology of professional ethical behaviour, and in particular the validity of the PQA model for the assessment and selection of medical and allied health professional students.

Our research team works across faculties within the University of Newcastle and collaborates with a number of other universities and medical schools in Australia, England, Scotland, Canada and Sweden.

Potential collaborative research is planned in Hong Kong and China.

Our current studies focus on:

• Potential differences between known groups (such as moral orientation in psychology, nursing, medical and other health professional students)
• The relationship between moral types and self-regulation
• Psychological health in medical students and physicians
• The predictive validity of PQA and its component tests in terms of medical education outcomes and professional behaviour.

We welcome any enquiry about potential collaborative research related to

• Assessment of applicants for, and selection into, professional programs
• The psychology of moral and ethical behaviour.
